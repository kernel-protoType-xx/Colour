# Colour Foundation Charter

## Mission

To build and maintain the world's most secure open source cryptographic vault standard — free for individuals, trusted by institutions, ready for the quantum era.

## Principles

**Open source forever.** The protocol is and will always be open source. No version of Colour Vault will ever be closed.

**Zero data custody.** The foundation will never operate infrastructure that processes or stores user key material, addresses, or identity data. This is architectural, not policy.

**Mathematical trust.** Security claims are backed by published code, independent audits, and formal verification — not promises.

**Quantum readiness.** All protocol decisions consider the post-quantum threat. No algorithm is added that weakens quantum resistance.

**Community governance.** Protocol decisions are made publicly. Foundation members participate in governance. Changes are documented transparently.

## Governance

The foundation is governed by its board and member institutions. Protocol changes require:

- Public proposal with technical justification
- 30-day community comment period
- Board approval
- Implementation with full test coverage

Security patches may be expedited with post-hoc disclosure.
